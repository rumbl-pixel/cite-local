# Zotero in 10 minutes (and how to never pay for storage)

Zotero does everything CiteLocal does, plus a live Word/Docs plugin and a PDF
library. Use it as your daily driver; use CiteLocal for quick one-off citations.

## Install (5 min)

1. **Zotero app** — https://www.zotero.org/download (Windows). Install, open,
   skip the account prompt for now (you can add it later; it's optional).
2. **Browser connector** — same download page, "Zotero Connector" for your
   browser (Chrome/Edge/Firefox). This is the one-click "save this page" button.
3. **Word plugin** — installs automatically with the app. Open Word → there's a
   **Zotero** tab in the ribbon. (If missing: Zotero → Edit → Settings → Cite →
   Word Processors → Reinstall.)

## First citation (2 min)

1. In your browser, open any article, journal page, book on Amazon/Google Books,
   or news page. Click the **Zotero Connector** button in the toolbar. The item
   drops into your Zotero library with full metadata.
2. Or in Zotero: click the **green "magic wand"** icon → paste a DOI, ISBN, or
   PMID → Enter. Full record appears.
3. Set your style: Zotero → Edit → Settings → **Cite** → get any of 10,000 styles.

## Put it in a document (2 min)

- **Word/LibreOffice**: Zotero tab → **Add/Edit Citation** → pick your style →
  search your library → insert. At the end, **Add/Edit Bibliography** builds the
  whole reference list and keeps it updated as you add more.
- **Google Docs**: the connector adds a **Zotero** menu — same flow.
- **Quick copy**: drag any item from Zotero into any text box, or select items →
  right-click → **Create Bibliography** → copy to clipboard.

## Never paying for storage — the important part

The **only** paid thing in Zotero is *cloud storage for synced file attachments*
(300 MB free). Your references, notes, and citations sync **free and unlimited**
forever. To keep attachments free too, pick one:

- **Don't sync files at all** — Settings → Sync → uncheck "Sync attachment files".
  Your PDFs stay on your disk, unlimited, they just don't upload. (Simplest.)
- **Linked files instead of stored copies** — Settings → Advanced → Files and
  Folders → "Linked Attachment Base Directory". Keep PDFs in your own
  folder/OneDrive/Dropbox; Zotero indexes them without touching its quota.
  Unlimited, free, and searchable.
- **WebDAV** — Settings → Sync → File Syncing → WebDAV → point at your own
  storage (NAS, a WebDAV host). Free, unlimited.

Do any one of these and you will never hit the storage wall or see an upgrade
prompt.

## When to use which

- **A paper, thesis, or anything with a real reference list** → Zotero (the Word
  plugin auto-builds and updates the bibliography as you write).
- **"I just need this one citation, fast"** → CiteLocal (paste URL/DOI → copy).
